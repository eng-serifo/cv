Intel launches processors.

Serifo Baldé

Intel launches Cascade Lake EP 48-core and Xeon E-2100 processors.

Intel, a leader in the chip manufacturing industry, recently announced that has two new processor lines: Cascade Lake and Xeon E-2100. Where Cascade Lake is expected to deliver top-notch performance, and Xeon processors E-2100 are for entry-level servers. Speaking of availability, Cascade Lake processors should be released in the first next year, while Intel Xeon E-2100 processors have been available since November 05, 2018. Lisa Spelman, Intel vice president and general manager of Intel Xeon products and product marketing data center, said the following about the new products:

“We remain highly focused on providing a wide range of load-optimized solutions in work that best meet our customers' system requirements. The addition of advanced performance processors Cascade Lake and the Xeon E-2100 processors to our line of Intel Xeon processors once again demonstrates our commitment to providing performance optimized for a wide range of customers. ”.

Specifically talking about the Cascade Lake processor, it is a very scalable that is geared towards high-performance computing, artificial intelligence and infrastructure-as-a-service workloads. It also includes 48 cores per CPU and 12 channels of DDR4 memory per socket, as per the company. In the Famous Linpack benchmark tests, it performed 1.21 times better than the Intel Xeon processor Scalable 8180 and 3.4 times better than AMD EPYC 7601.

Whereas talking about the Intel Xeon E-2100 series, these processors are aimed at small and medium businesses and cloud service providers. They offer hardware-based features that provide additional security for data and applications. Intel also said the new lineup is also capable of withstanding heavy workloads. suitable for entry-level servers.
