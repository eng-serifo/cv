W3C: WebAuthn is now an official web standard

Serifo Baldé

Newly Approved WebAuthn Lets You Login to All Your Accounts Without a Password.

Say goodbye to passwords and move on to web authentication as the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) and the FIDO Alliance announced on Monday the finalization of a web standard for logins secure and passwordless. It has now been approved as the official web standard. "This advancement is a big step towards making the web more secure - and usable - for users in around the world," the W3C said in its press release. The Web Authentication (WebAuthn) specification allows users to log in to their Internet accounts using your preferred device without having to remember passwords. Instead In addition, users can log in using biometric data such as fingerprints, keys USB security devices or devices such as smartphones or watches.

The W3C claims that this will make websites more secure and make passwords more secure. he is already supported on Windows 10, Android and Google Chrome web browsers, Mozilla Firefox, Microsoft Edge and Apple Safari (Preview). The W3C recommends that websites adopt the new standard to create a safer environment for users and allow them to log in more easily, quickly and securely: "Now is the time for web services and businesses to embrace WebAuthn to go beyond passwords vulnerable and help web users improve the safety of their online experiences," said Jeff Jaffe, CEO of the W3C. “The W3C Recommendation sets forth a guideline for interoperability for the entire web, setting consistent expectations for users of the web and the sites they visit. The W3C is working to implement this best practice in your own website.”

In addition, the W3C believes that WebAuthn will eradicate many issues related to traditional authentication methods. “It is common knowledge that passwords have outgrown their effectiveness. It's not just passwords stolen, weak, or default behind 81% of data breaches, they are a waste of time and resources. While traditional multi-factor authentication (MFA) solutions, such as SMS messages, add another layer of security, they are still vulnerable to e-mail attacks. phishing, are not simple to use and suffer from low adherence rates. With WebAuthn, the global tech community came together to provide a shared solution to the problem shared password ”.

On the other hand, FIDO keys have many advantages over passwords and address all the problems with traditional authentication such as:

Security: FIDO2 cryptographic login credentials are unique across all websites, biometrics or other secrets such as passwords never leave the user and are never stored on a server. This security model eliminates the risks of phishing, all forms of password theft and replay attacks.

Convenience: Users log in with convenient methods such as fingerprint, cameras, FIDO security keys or your personal mobile device.

Privacy : As FIDO keys are unique to each internet site, they are not can be used to track you on websites.

Scalability: Websites can enable FIDO2 via simple API calls across all supported browsers and platforms on billions of devices that consumers use every day.

"Web authentication as the official web standard is the culmination of many years industry collaboration to develop a practical solution for stronger authentication on the Web," said Brett McDowell, Director executive of the FIDO Alliance. "With this milestone, we are moving into a new era of FIDO authentication protection ubiquitous and supported by hardware for everyone who uses the Internet". The announcement by WebAuthn of the W3C and the FIDO Alliance is, we hope, a step towards achieving passwordless logins in websites. We expect to see many web services implementing WebAuthn in the coming months and, eventually a use broader of it on the Web as a whole.
